What is Prototyping?

What is Prototyping?

A prototype is a model version of a product. It is used as an early, inexpensive sample of a product that helps to test its features or identify defects so improvements can be made to its final version. Prototypes provides the opportunity to gather valuable feedback from stakeholders, partners or customers about their product.(Creately, 2019)

There are two prototyping methods you can choose which are low-fidelity prototype or high-fidelity prototype.

What is the difference between low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes?

Low-fidelity types are prototypes that are the incomplete version of the final product. Usually low-fidelity types are used for gathering user feedback in the early stage and doesnâ€™t take much time and effort, Whereas High-fidelity types take more time and effort and usually is more interactive and highly functional. (Creately, 2019)

Low-fidelity prototypes

Wireframes are used to represent the basic structure of a website/app. Seen like a blueprint, highlighting layout of elements on pages and its functionality.

Storyboards is a method that helps the user visualize your product and how they would interact with it

High-fidelity prototypes

UI Mockups are more detailed version of wireframes. Represents color schemes, fonttypes and more

Physical Models are also high-fidelity prototypes made of different materials to create a model and represent the final shape, look, and feel of the product. Can use cupboards, clay, rubber, wood.

Why is prototyping important?

The significance of prototyping lies on how your stakeholders and users feel about your product, designers learn about them and identify the areas for improvement of your product in the early stages so that the outcome would be magnificent.
