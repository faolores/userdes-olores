G1

What is User Design?

What is Design?

Design is the preliminary sketch or the plan when constructing a structure of an object or system. I think when considering designs, you think of its users, and how everything is in harmony. (Riley)

What is User Design?

User Design is an iterative design process in which designers focus on the users and their needs in each phase of the design process. I think user design is like a step-by-step process where designers try to solve the problem found by users. (Interaction Design Foundation)

What is the difference between User Experience Design and User Interface Design? Why does it matter?

UI design is a subset of UX design. UX design is mostly the whole design which includes branding, usability and its function. UI design focuses on the user interface and on how it could be user-friendly also focuses on aesthetic of the design. Both are important since if there is only UI design then it would not be focused much because of unknown branding, and if UX design is only present, then it will not be aesthetic and pleasing to other users. I feel like UX is mainly for the users and UI for the designers, the relationship differ because they focus on different goals yet there is a connection. (Interaction Design Foundation)

Who are the users? Why are they important?

Usually, the users are the community that often uses the brand sometimes there are also betatesters, stakeholders, basically the people that have interacted with the design. They are important because they can give reviews and on what is good and what can be improved. They are also the people that can make your design popular if they find it pleasant.
